Student Program Designer · Community Health Project · Year 8 HAPE

I'm a student based in Wyndham, Victoria. This project was developed as part of a Year 8 community health assignment - the goal was to design a program that responded to a real, documented health need in the local area, rather than a made-up one.

I researched Wyndham's existing health programs, identified a specific gap in youth-focused services, and built a program concept around that gap - drawing on the Municipal Public Health and Wellbeing Plan (2021–2025), local community health data, and reports from organisations like IPC Health and the Wyndham Health Foundation.

The WynHealth Rover is a proposal, not a funded organisation. But the problem it responds to is real, and I designed it to be realistic enough that it could actually work.

I noticed Wyndham has over 50 community health programs but almost none for youth - confirmed by the Municipal Health Plan and 2024-2026 reports showing this gap. The mobile rover model solves the main barriers: teens won't travel, pay, or commit to appointments. A 15-minute drop-in format fits real teen attention spans and removes those barriers at once - coming to where they already are, costing nothing, and requiring zero prior commitment.
