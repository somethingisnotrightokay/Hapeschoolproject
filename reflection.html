Why I Created This

I kept wondering why Wyndham's got 50+ community health programs but barely anything for teens like me. So I dug into the actual listings. IPC Health, community centres, council initiatives. and it's all senior walking groups, adult cooking classes, and carer support. Nothing at all for my age bracket.

I picked Wyndham because(It;s where the project is supposed to happen), and the data made the gap impossible to ignore. The Municipal Health Plan and those 2024-2026 health reports just say there's a youth program drought outside schools. So this wasn't just me guessing, it's well documented reality.

The mobile rover idea hit me when I thought about why most people my age ignore existing health services. Spoiler: it's not that we don't care about health - it's that most services make you travel, book appointments, or pay money. The Rover fixes that by coming to you, being 100% free, and needing zero commitment.

The 15-minute session length came from being brutally honest about teen attention spans. Something boring like an hour long workshop just wouldn't work. But something quick, active, and actually interesting? That would work. So I built everything around: what's the most useful stuff we can squeeze into 15 minutes covering movement, nutrition, AND behaviour change?

I'm not claiming the Rover solves every single Wyndham health issue (check the Goals page for the real challenges). But it's tackling a genuine problem that needed solving, which is why I went all-in on this for the assignment.

A Year 8 student living in Wyndham, Victoria. The Rover was my idea for my HAPE assignment where I had to design an actual community health program, and not just something low-effort like a typed report.

I researched what Wyndham already offers, spotted the teen-sized hole in their services, and built the Rover concept around filling that gap. Using the council's health plan (2021-2025), local stats, and reports from IPC Health plus the Wyndham Health Foundation.

Important: it's still just a proposal (no actual van yet), but the problem's 100% real and I made sure the idea could work in reality if someone ever decided to fund it.
