Why I Created This

This project started with a question I kept coming back to: if Wyndham already has over 50 community health programs, why do almost none of them seem to be built for young people specifically? I started going through the actual list — the real programs from IPC Health, the community centres, the council initiatives — and the pattern was clear. Walking groups for seniors. Cooking classes for adults. Support programs for carers. Almost nothing for someone my age.

I chose Wyndham specifically because it's where I live, and because the data made the gap easy to see once I started looking. The Municipal Health Plan and the 2024–2026 community health reports both mention the lack of youth-focused programs outside of schools — it wasn't just my impression, it was documented. That made the project feel like it was about something real, not just a made-up scenario for an assignment.

The mobile rover model came from thinking about why young people don't use the services that do exist. It's usually not that they don't care about their health — it's that the services require them to travel somewhere, book an appointment, or pay money. A program that comes to where people already are, costs nothing, and doesn't require any prior commitment removes all three of those barriers at once. That felt like a more useful design than building another community centre program that people have to choose to go to.

The 35-minute session format came from being honest about what a teenager will actually sit through. A two-hour health workshop is not going to work at a park on a Wednesday lunch break. Something short, active, and genuinely interesting might. So the whole structure was built around that constraint — what's the most useful thing you can do with 35 minutes if you're trying to cover movement, nutrition, and behaviour change all in one go?

I don't think this program is perfect — the challenges section on the Goals page reflects that. But I do think it's solving a real problem in a way that's actually suited to the people it's meant to help. That felt worth designing properly.

About the Creator

Student Program Designer · Community Health Project · Year 8 HPE

I'm a student based in Wyndham, Victoria. This project was developed as part of a Year 8 community health assignment — the goal was to design a program that responded to a real, documented health need in the local area, rather than a made-up one.

I researched Wyndham's existing health programs, identified a specific gap in youth-focused services, and built a program concept around that gap — drawing on the Municipal Public Health and Wellbeing Plan (2021–2025), local community health data, and reports from organisations like IPC Health and the Wyndham Health Foundation.

The WynHealth Rover is a proposal, not a funded organisation. But the problem it responds to is real, and I designed it to be realistic enough that it could actually work.
