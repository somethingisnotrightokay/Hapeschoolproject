The Session Flow

1
⚡

Movement Burst

Squat-reach-jump sequences, fast laps, shadow boxing. Gets everyone activated and ready.

5 min

Why: exercise before learning = better focus. Also it's more fun than sitting down immediately

2
🥦

Nutrition Skill

Hands-on food activity — label reading (3 packages max), sugar detective challenge, or balanced plate build. Rotates each visit.

5 min

Why: doing something is more memorable than being told about it. Nobody remembers a slide about vegetables

3
🧠

Behaviour Change

One focused lesson on sleep, screens, stress, vaping, or relationships. Single-topic by design.

5 min

Why: trying to teach five things at once means nobody remembers any of them

4
🎮

Movement Game

Reaction tag, team relays, mirror-me. Second heart-rate spike. Ends high, not flat.

5 min

Why: people come back to things they enjoyed. Ending on a game > ending on a worksheet

5
📋

Habit Challenge (optional, but your mum will ask about it)

Streak Card or QR-code habit tracker to continue the change at home.

0-5 min (optional)

Why: one session doesn't build a habit. This keeps it going at home without being annoying about it

Total designed session time: 15-20 minutes

Why This Structure Works

Science-Backed Sequencing

Movement first → primes brain for learning (Ratey, 2008). Nutrition hands-on → creates muscle memory. Behaviour change single-topic → prevents cognitive overload. Game ending → leaves positive association. Habit tracker → bridges school-to-home.

Teen Attention Economics

15-20 min respects actual adolescent focus spans (Wyndham Youth Survey 2023: 78% disengage after 20 min). Each segment varies stimulus type (physical → cognitive → social → playful → reflective) to combat boredom.

Zero-Setup Design

All activities use: bodyweight only, supermarket packaging, or printed cards. No gyms, kitchens, or tech required. Works on ovals, undercover areas, or library floors. Rain plan: shift to covered zones with same timing.
