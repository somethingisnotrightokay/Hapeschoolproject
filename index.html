The Health Issue

This isn't made up. These are real, documented problems affecting real young people in Wyndham specifically.

The World Health Organization identifies physical inactivity as one of the leading risk factors for global health problems. According to WHO, most adolescents don't get enough physical activity — and the gap is even larger for girls and young women, who are consistently less active than boys. Older adults and people with disabilities are also at higher risk of missing out on the benefits of exercise.

In Wyndham specifically, young people face a combination of these challenges that are well-documented but poorly addressed by existing services. Low physical activity, poor nutrition habits, and increasing rates of stress and anxiety are consistently identified in local health data — yet almost all community programs are built for adults, seniors, or families.

Wyndham's own Municipal Public Health and Wellbeing Plan (2021–2025) acknowledges this gap. The 2024–2026 Community Health and Wellbeing Report specifically calls out the lack of youth-focused mental health programs outside of schools.

The problem isn't just a shortage of programs — it's a shortage of programs that are free, mobile, gender-inclusive, culturally accessible, and actually relevant to young people's lives. WynHealth Rover is designed to address all of those gaps at once, specifically targeting the 13–18 age group where physical activity habits are still being formed.

"There is less visibility of youth-specific mental health initiatives outside of school settings. Expanding peer support for young people could address this gap."

— Wyndham Community Health & Wellbeing Report, 2024–2026

🏃 Low Physical Activity

Only 1 in 5 Australian teens meets the recommended 60 minutes of moderate-vigorous activity per day. Sedentary time has gone up significantly since 2020.

🍔 Poor Nutrition Habits

Most Australian teenagers consume well above the recommended daily intake of added sugar, and fall short on fruit, vegetables, and fibre.

🧠 Youth Mental Health

1 in 7 Australian kids experience a mental health condition. Most conditions emerge before age 25 — making early community-level support genuinely important.

🚧 Access Barriers

Cost, transport, cultural unfamiliarity, and the need for referrals all reduce uptake of existing health services — especially in Wyndham's diverse communities.
