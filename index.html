The Health Issue

Real problems, documented in real reports. I didn't make this up — which honestly made the assignment much easier.

Young people in Wyndham face a combination of health challenges that are well-documented but poorly addressed by existing services. Low physical activity, poor nutrition habits, and increasing stress and anxiety are identified in national and local health data — yet almost all community programs are built for adults, seniors, or families.

Wyndham's own Municipal Public Health and Wellbeing Plan (2021–2025) acknowledges this gap. The 2024–2026 Community Health and Wellbeing Report specifically calls out the lack of youth-focused programs outside schools.

The problem isn't just a shortage of programs — it's a shortage of programs that are free, mobile, and actually relevant to young people's lives. Most existing options are fixed-location, need a referral, or cost money. So this is the fix.

"There is less visibility of youth-specific mental health initiatives outside of school settings. Expanding peer support for young people could address this gap."

— Wyndham Community Health & Wellbeing Report, 2024–2026

🏃 Low Physical Activity

Only 1 in 5 Australian teens meets the recommended 60 minutes of activity per day. Sedentary time has gone up since 2020. In case you needed more bad news.

🍔 Poor Nutrition Habits

Most Australian teenagers consume way above the recommended daily intake of added sugar. The Rover's sugar detective activity exists specifically because of this statistic.

🧠 Youth Mental Health

1 in 7 Australian kids experience a mental health condition. 75% of conditions emerge before age 25. Look to your left. Look to your right. Statistically, one of you is struggling. It's probably you.

🚧 Access Barriers

Cost, transport, cultural unfamiliarity, and needing a referral all reduce uptake. Especially in Wyndham's diverse communities. The Rover removes all of these at once.
