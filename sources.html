Sources + References

Every claim on this website is based on one of these sources. Listed in the order they appear across the site. All accessed 2025.

📍 Wyndham Local Sources

Wyndham Community Health & Wellbeing Report 2024–2026

Source of the direct quote used about the lack of youth-specific mental health initiatives outside school settings. Also referenced for youth program gap data and CALD population statistics.

wyndham.vic.gov.au ↗

Wyndham City Municipal Public Health and Wellbeing Plan 2021–2025

Referenced throughout for the identification of cost as a barrier to health access, the documented gap in youth programs, and Wyndham's health priorities that the Rover aligns with.

wyndham.vic.gov.au ↗

IPC Health — Wyndham Services Overview

Used to identify existing community health programs in Wyndham and the types of populations they serve (adults, seniors, carers). IPC Health is the primary health service provider in the area.

ipchealth.com.au ↗

Wyndham Health Foundation

Referenced as a local funding body and community health organisation. Program funding strategy in the goals section identifies the Foundation as a potential grant source.

wyndhamhealthfoundation.org.au ↗

🇦🇺 Australian Health Guidelines

Australian Government Department of Health — Physical Activity and Sedentary Behaviour Guidelines (2021)

Source for the 60 minutes/day physical activity guideline for teenagers, the muscle-strengthening recommendation, screen time limits, and sedentary behaviour guidance. Also referenced for the statistic that only 1 in 5 Australian adolescents meets the activity guideline.

health.gov.au ↗

Australian Government Department of Health — Sleep Health Guidelines

Source for the 8–10 hours of sleep per night recommendation for teenagers aged 13–17. Referenced in the guidelines page and the behaviour change lesson content.

health.gov.au ↗

Australian Institute of Health and Welfare (AIHW) — Young Australians: Their Health and Wellbeing (2023)

Source for the 1 in 7 Australian children and adolescents experiencing a mental health condition statistic. Also referenced for the 75% of mental health conditions emerging before age 25 figure.

aihw.gov.au ↗

Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS) — National Health Survey: Physical Activity (2022)

Referenced for the statistic that most Australian teenagers consume well above the recommended daily intake of added sugar, and for general population-level nutrition data.

abs.gov.au ↗

🌍 International + Other Sources

World Health Organization (WHO) — Physical Activity Fact Sheet (2022)

Referenced in the assignment task sheet itself as the basis for the issue statement. Also cited for global context on physical activity levels and the health impacts of inactivity.

who.int ↗

Heart Foundation Australia — Heart Health for Young People

Referenced as a community partner organisation and for information on cardiovascular benefits of physical activity. The Heart Foundation Walking Groups are listed as an aligned Wyndham organisation.

heartfoundation.org.au ↗

Biddle, S. J. H., & Asare, M. (2011). Physical activity and mental health in children and adolescents: a review of reviews. British Journal of Sports Medicine, 45(11), 886–895.

Cited for the documented relationship between physical activity and reduced stress and anxiety in adolescents. Used in the benefits of physical activity section and the session structure rationale.

Ratey, J. J. (2008). Spark: The Revolutionary New Science of Exercise and the Brain. Little, Brown.

Referenced for the finding that physical activity before learning improves focus and retention — the basis for structuring the Rover session with movement first, content second.
