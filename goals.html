Program Goals

Specific, measurable outcomes for a 12-week pilot. Each one has a defined target and a way to measure it. This is the part of the assignment where you have to put actual numbers. I put actual numbers.

Goal 01

Increase Physical Activity

Participants report at least a 20% increase in days per week with moderate physical activity, measured through pre- and post-program surveys.

Target: 20% increase over 12 weeks
Measured by: self-report activity survey (pre/post)
Goal 02

Improve Nutrition Knowledge

Participants show improvement in nutrition knowledge scores between a pre-program quiz and the same quiz at the final session.

Target: average score improvement of 25%
Measured by: identical pre/post knowledge quiz
Goal 03

Achieve Repeat Attendance

At least 60% of first-session participants return for a second or third session in the 12 weeks. If people come back, it's working. If they don't, it's not.

Target: 60% return rate
Measured by: attendance sign-in records
Goal 04

Build Confidence in Healthy Habits

Participants self-report feeling more confident in their ability to make healthy choices and manage stress — measured on a 1–5 scale.

Target: average increase of 1.5 / 5
Measured by: confidence rating scale (pre/post)

The Evaluation Plan

Simple, practical ways to measure whether the program is working. Not a 60-page research report — just useful data collected during normal sessions. The assignment asked for an evaluation plan. This is the evaluation plan.

📋

Pre & Post Surveys

A 10-question survey at the first and final session of each 12-week cycle. Covers activity frequency, nutrition knowledge, and self-rated confidence. Results are compared to measure change.

📊

Attendance Tracking

A sign-in sheet at each session. Tracks who attends and whether they're returning or new. Return rate is calculated at the end of each cycle. Simple. Effective.

✏️

Session Feedback Forms

Three questions at the end of each session: what did you learn, what did you enjoy, what could be better. Ongoing feedback — not just end-of-program.

📱

Habit Tracker Check-Ins

Participants with a Streak Card report back at their next session on whether they attempted the habit at home. Measures behaviour change beyond the session itself.

💬

Verbal Reflection

At the start of each session, 2–3 participants share one thing they remember from last time. Quick, informal measure of knowledge retention without a formal test.

📝

End-of-Cycle Summary

At the end of each 12 weeks, all data is compiled into a one-page summary. Attendance trends, survey score changes, recurring feedback. Used to improve the next cycle.

Challenges and How to Address Them

A realistic program has to account for what could go wrong. These are genuine limitations — not made up to fill a section, even though this section is required by the rubric.

🌧️ Weather

A mobile program running in parks and ovals is affected by rain and extreme heat — both common in Wyndham.

Response

Sessions are designed to be adaptable to indoor spaces at short notice. School visits use existing covered areas. Weather contingency confirmed with each venue in advance.

🏫 School Timetable Conflicts

Schools have structured schedules. Finding consistent time — especially during assessment periods — is genuinely difficult.

Response

35-minute format fits within a lunch break. Initial outreach targets wellbeing coordinators who already allocate health time. Non-school visits are an alternative.

💰 Funding

Running a mobile unit requires ongoing money for equipment, transport, and facilitators. Without funding, the program can't run past a pilot.

Response

The program aligns with Wyndham City Council's health priorities — a realistic path to local grants. The Wyndham Health Foundation and IPC Health both fund community health initiatives here. Volunteer youth leaders reduce staffing costs over time.

👥 Student Engagement

Young people don't show up just because something is available — especially if it feels like a health lesson disguised as free time.

Response

Sessions prioritise movement and games over instruction. Youth leaders from the community are more effective at driving participation than adults alone.

🌏 Cultural Inclusivity

With 40%+ of Wyndham from diverse backgrounds, a one-size-fits-all approach risks being irrelevant for many participants.

Response

Nutrition activities use examples from multiple cuisines. No cultural knowledge assumed. Facilitator training includes cultural competency.

🏃 Students who think PE is already enough

Some students will argue they already exercise enough through PE or sport and don't need this.

Response

PE doesn't cover nutrition, sleep, screen time, stress, or harm prevention. The Rover isn't a replacement for PE — it covers everything PE doesn't. Also, 1 in 5 teens meets the physical activity guideline. It's probably not you.
